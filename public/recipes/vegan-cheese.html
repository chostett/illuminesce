Vegan Cheese

Spreadable vegan cheddar cheese for mac n' cheese, grilled cheese, and dips.

Ingredients

Makes enough for three cups. Will keep for ~2 weeks.
  • 1 1/3 cup cashews
  • 1 cup thinly sliced carrots
  • Hot water for soaking
  • 1/2 cup tapioca starch
  • 6 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 3 tsp apple cider vinegar (りんご酢)
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • Turmeric for coloring

Suggested Tools

  • Heat-tolerant bowl for soaking
  • Blender or food processor
  • Stovetop
  • Frypan

How to Make

30 minutes prep, 15 minutes in kitchen.
  1. Soak the cashews and carrots in boiling water for 30 minutes.
  2. Put the softened cashews and carrots in a blender or food processor with water and add tapioca starch, nutritional yeast, apple cider vinegar, salt, garlic, mustard and turmeric.
  3. Blend until creamy.
  4. Taste test the mixture and adjust mustard/garlic/salt/nutritional yeast as necessary.
  5. Put in a skillet and cook on medium low until mixture thickens.
  6. Should be good for 3 cups worth of vegan cheese. Lasts about two weeks.

Author Notes

I hate it when recipes tell you a story first before you have a chance to try it out, so I put the story here for those to enjoy after the recipe.

I first discovered this recipe via The Minimalist Baker when I was looking for a vegan cheese recipe that would work well with macaroni and cheese. My partner and I love Kenji Lopez-Alt's stovetop mac and cheese recipe and I wanted to find a vegan cheese good enough to make that recipe.

Getting nutritional yeast and tapioca starch proved to be a challenge - it can't be bought at traditional grocery stores, but I did find nutritional yeast at National Azabu and tapioca starch at Tomiz. They are on Amazon, though. Everything else can usually be found at my local market - I have a local vendor I source the cashew nuts from and he sells them in bulk.

Warning: this cheese is very addictive. I eat so many grilled cheese sandwiches and veggie dip plates with this.
