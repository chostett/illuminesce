IWAKAN Volume 04 特集 多様性？

The Diversity? Edition

■English summary | 英語の概要

To portray diversity is to portray the individual.

Who portrays "diversity?" Is "diversity" people who are "not normal" living together with the "normal" majority? This issue questions the "discomfort" of diverse representations that include both the power of hope and the power of perpetration when they depict diversity such as the LGBTQ+ community and racial identities.

This issue incudes interviews with ALOK, a prominent activist in American gender issues, and Sebastian Lifshitz, director of the documentary Little Girl. There are also columns on "masculinity" and the representation of family as well as a trilogy that discusses true consensus in the production process. We also include original advertisements with a twist.

CJ Note: This is a labor of love and obsession with documenting queer and alternate voices in Japan. If you are a translator/native speaker and feel I've made an error, please contact me at (illuminesce @ pm (.) me).

■日本語の原板 | Original Japanese (via the online store)

多様性を描くことは、個人を描くこと

“多様性“を描くのは誰か？普通でないとされる人々がマジョリティと共にあることが “多様性“なのか?LGBTQ+や人種といった多様なアイデンディティを描く希望と加害性、どちらの力も併せ持つ多様性表象に“違和感“を問いかける。アメリカのジェンダー問題における著名なアクティビストであるALOKやドキュメンタリー映画『リトル・ガール』のセバスチャン・リフシッツ監督インタビュー。“男らしさ“や家族の表象にまつわるコラム、制作現場における真の合意を議論する鼎談のほか、ひねりの効いたオリジナル広告も。
